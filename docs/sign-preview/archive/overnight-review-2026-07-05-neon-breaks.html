What the sprint established

The panel vector
Element 6 is the logo’s left panel — bolt and X are one fused outline. It ships with one painted neon break (bolt-tail tip) and two kiss-junctions that fuse in our band width. Extracted at 505 mm ink height → panel stands 2.06× the letter cap, the deployed proportion.
Element 5’s real job
The “wrong bolt” of the last sprint is the red inner’s slim-bolt shape. The billboard draws that red as a single-stroke zigzag tube — four vertices, not an outline — which is exactly what a 22 mm channel can build.
Red placement
Hand-fit to the billboard, then pushed to every clearance limit with the new audit tool. Final: zero sub-26 mm contacts against the yellow. It crosses no plate seam.
Board & plates
410×550 face. A single vertical seam is geometrically impossible (the X’s legs are near-vertical), so seams are piecewise: y=255 full width, top row splits at x=126, bottom at x=153. Four rectangular plates, all inside the validated 316×295 both-nozzle zone. 7 engineered pullback breaks; pullbacks are perpendicular-true (13/sin θ).
Pixels & power
141 px @20 mm (119 yellow / 22 red), relaxation-solved, 3 snug pairs. Sign total 595 px vs the 150 W PSU’s ~600 full-white edge — the locked “cap ~80%” rule covers it.

Where physics said no (so you don’t re-litigate at 7am)

Red bar can’t tilt
The billboard’s bar rises to the right. Between the X’s top bar (y≈322) and the notch shelf (y=375), the legal corridor for a 22 mm channel is exactly zero at the shelf’s x-range — the bar threads the top-bar gap at y≈349, flat. An 18 mm red channel would open the corridor ~4 mm — see iterations.
Red tail stops at y≈239
The wedge corridor between the bolt-tail edges pinches below 52 mm interior. The billboard’s own red tip measures out at ~59%% panel height — ours lands within ~15 mm of that. Genuinely close.
Y-seam crosses the X’s waist
Bed depth (295) pins the seam at y=255; the red tip pins it from above. Its 4 breaks line up across the waist — the same engineered-break look you approved on the old 2-plate C1, and period-correct for segmented neon.

Iterations run in spirit — verdicts

A · as built Single-stroke red, 22 mm everywhere, 20 mm pitch, 4 plates. BUILT & PACKED
B · thin red (18mm) Second cross-section for the red channel. Buys ~10 mm deeper tail + ~2° of bar tilt; costs a second SCAD variant, 14 mm interior (flange 13.6 = snip every red bullet), and a mixed-width look. NOT WORTH IT
C · 24mm pitch Board drops 141→120 px, sign 595→574. Only matters if you want full-white at 100%% on one PSU. Rerun boltboard.py --pitch 24 + rebuild if so. HOLD AS OPTION
D · double-run fat red The old C1’s two-parallel-run red needs 74 mm of corridor at the bar/tail (26 red-red + 2×24 to yellow); the fused composition offers 52 max. Dies at the bar. GEOMETRICALLY DEAD

Wiring chain — one data line, two extension jumpers

The bullets are addressable, so color zones are software — the whole board runs as one 141-px chain. Order: arc order within each tube run, then nearest-run-end greedy. 140 links, max 130 mm; only 2 links exceed the 4-inch string (dashed red, chain idx 91 and 118) — use two of the spare extension jumpers there. bolt_pixmap.json now carries chain per pixel alongside color zone and plate, so the controller map is a straight read. Numbers on the drawing mark every 10th chain position; IN marks the feed end (bottom of the board).
