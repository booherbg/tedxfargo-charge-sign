White PETG splice straps replace the y-seam wood rail — the plates screw into captive M4 nuts.

  1. Plates face-down on a flat table, butted B1|B2, B3|B4, rows together.
  2. Drop M4 nuts in the strap pockets, set straps on the seams, screw from the front.
  3. Mount the joined panel to the frame by the perimeter wood screws (no y-seam rail).
  4. Press pixels in from behind — near seams they pass through the strap holes (use the pusher; the two on-seam pixels seat into the strap collars, 2 mm deeper by design).
  5. Chain per bolt_pixmap.json (jumpers at links 86, 107); wires always route behind the straps — nothing gets pinched.
Leg sockets stay empty for now. If the mounted panel flexes against the back skin, print Ø10 friction-fit legs at the measured plenum depth and press them into the three sockets — the "pizza saver" upgrade path.
